BLCP Power Plant or BLCP Power Company Limited is a Thai joint venture company (50:50)
between Banpu Group (Banpu Coal Power Company Limited and Banpu Public Company Limited)
and Electricity Generating Public Company Limited (EGCO Group)

BLCP Power Plant operates electricity generation business
using modern equipment and production technology
along with strict environmental management
to comply with government standards
while considering community feelings and needs as priority
BLCP Power Plant uses high-quality coal
specifically bituminous coal as fuel
which is mostly imported from Australia
with a total generating capacity of 1,434 megawatts

BLCP Power Plant began electricity generation for the first unit (717 megawatts)
on October 1, 2006 and the second unit (717 megawatts) in February 2007
which is in accordance with the scheduled timeline under the Power Development Plan
(Power Development Plan; PDP) of the Electricity Generating Authority of Thailand (EGAT)
and distributes electricity to EGAT under the Independent Power Producer
(Independent Power Producer; IPP) project according to the Cabinet Resolution of 1994

To support the government policy in promoting and supporting the private sector's role
in participating in electricity business. The company signed a power purchase agreement with EGAT
on November 19, 1997 for a total period of 25 years

BLCP Power Plant is located in Map Ta Phut Industrial Port Area
Phase 2, which is a deep-sea port. The company signed a lease agreement
with the Industrial Estate Authority of Thailand (IEAT)
for approximately 600 rai from June 30, 2000
for a total lease period of 30 years

This power plant has received various licenses for construction
and operation, and has complied with standards, rules
and regulations similar to other industrial factories
located in Map Ta Phut Industrial Estate, Map Ta Phut Sub-district
Rayong Province

BLCP Power Station signed the
Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)
with the Electricity Generating Authority of Thailand (EGAT)
on November 19, 1997
(25-year contract term)

BLCP Power Plant is considered to have large electricity generation capacity
ranking 5th in the country. The generation capacity and operational commencement
of both generation units are consistent with Thailand's Power Development Plan
(Power Development Plan-PDP)

This is to help strengthen electrical system stability (System Stability) and to maintain the balance between production volume
and electricity consumption of the country (Reserve Margin). In addition, BLCP Power Plant has production cost per unit
at a level that will help reduce the country's overall production costs (Cost Stability) as well as help slow down retail price increases
of domestic electricity tariffs (Price Stability) amid the current expensive and volatile oil situation in the global market

Importantly, BLCP Power Plant also helps promote the fuel diversification policy to make it diverse for the country
This is to help reduce risks and prevent concentration of fuel types in the country's future electricity generation
(Fuel Diversification Policy)

Following government policy that promotes energy conservation and environmental protection, including policy to solve the palm oil surplus problem,
BLCP Power Plant is another operator that supports government policy to jointly maintain a good environment
BLCP Power Plant ordered B20 diesel oil totaling 1,367,000 liters in 2 batches in late 2019
and on April 29, 2020

When calculated as a proportion of pure palm oil biodiesel totaling 273,400 liters to replace fuel use for
start up and shutdown of the power plant from previously using regular diesel oil to comply with the Ministry of Energy's emphasis
on promoting energy policy for people at all levels according to the Energy For All policy and to promote increased use of biodiesel
mixed in diesel oil while also helping palm oil farmers

BLCP Power Plant

BLCP Power Plant continues to develop continuously with social responsibility. In 2020
it responded to government policy and the Industrial Estate Authority of Thailand (IEAT) to reduce raw water usage by up to 100%
so that farmers and Rayong residents have sufficient water during drought crisis. BLCP Power Plant has implemented measures
to reduce water usage and has a policy to produce tap water and raw water from seawater (Reverse Osmosis Sea water Desalination
Plant: RO Water) for use within the power plant even though the operation has relatively higher costs than using raw water

In January 2020, raw water usage was 20,205 cubic meters
February 2020 decreased to 5,054 cubic meters, and from February 15, 2020 onwards, BLCP Power Plant notified its intention to stop receiving raw water
from East Water by converting seawater into fresh water (RO Water)

To replace raw water used in the electricity generation process including use for utilities and watering plants, which are green areas
within the entire power plant resulting in the ability to reduce raw water usage from East Water to 'zero' until present
