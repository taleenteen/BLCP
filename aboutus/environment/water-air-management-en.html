Air Impact Reduction Measures

BLCP Power Plant prioritizes environmental management as a top priority and confirms that it meets
environmental standards and complies with government regulations to help improve the environment.
BLCP Power Plant uses proven technology from around the world and is environmentally friendly
by installing various pollution reduction equipment, including:

•Installation of low NOx burners to reduce nitrogen oxide emissions

•Installation of Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for dust collection

•Installation of Flue Gas Desulphurization (FGD) system for sulfur dioxide removal

•200-meter high chimney stack

For air monitoring, BLCP Power Plant has installed Continuous Emission Monitoring System (CEMS)
at the stack and 4 Air Quality Monitoring Stations (AQMS) around the BLCP Power Plant.

BLCP Power Plant
controls the sulfur content in coal at a low level of 0.27-0.7%
with an average of 0.45% per year.
We also control pollutant emissions to the atmosphere to be better than government standards
as follows:

•Particulate matter 43 mg/m3
from PCD standard of 120 mg/m3
which is 64% better than standard

•Sulfur dioxide (SO2) as specified in EIA
is 262 ppm from PCD standard of 320 ppm
18% better than standard

•Nitrogen oxides (NOx) 241 ppm
from PCD standard of 350 ppm
31% better than standard

Water Impact Reduction Measures

Water used in the power plant is divided into 2 types:

• Fresh Water
(Water used in production processes, port, coal yard, and office) by:
* Providing Waste Water Treatment Plant
* Water from port and coal yard is recycled for reuse

• Sea Water

Pumped in for cooling and sulfur dioxide treatment system. Two-layer screens
(Traveling Screen and Bar Screen) are installed to prevent marine life from entering the cooling system.
The seawater intake velocity for cooling is controlled not to exceed 0.3 meters per second.
These screens help marine life that may enter to easily exit the cooling water channel.
In addition, an aeration basin is installed at the cooling water discharge point to adjust temperature
and pH before discharge to the sea (temperature < 40Â°C and pH ~ 7).
Regular system inspection and annual equipment maintenance plans are conducted,
including continuous monitoring of water quality and temperature around BLCP Power Plant.

Coal Ash

Coal ash is managed as follows:
Fly Ash from all combustion processes is sold to cement industries
as an ingredient in production and helps reduce raw material costs.
Ash from bituminous coal is considered high-quality coal ash
and is popular both domestically and internationally.
It is used in cement mixing for constructing Khlong Tha Dan Dam
in Nakhon Nayok Province, which is a royal initiative project.
Therefore, BLCP Power Plant does not need to landfill coal ash waste.

For Bottom Ash, in February 2025, a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) was signed
between BLCP Power Plant and Faculty of Architecture, Chulalongkorn University
to develop "Temperature-Reducing Bricks" from bottom ash generated from electricity production.
The goal is to reduce waste and create products beneficial to society and the environment.

The jointly developed temperature-reducing bricks have special properties to help reduce
indoor and surrounding temperatures, save energy, and are environmentally friendly.
This also aligns with the Circular Economy concept that emphasizes efficient and sustainable resource use.

Coal Handling Port

Bituminous coal, mostly imported from Australia, is transported by ship to BLCP Power Plant's
deep-sea port. BLCP Power Plant prioritizes environmental protection measures
by inspecting the quality of imported bituminous coal for every shipment.

Coal handling into the power plant and coal yard is done using conveyor belts with wind shields
covering the surroundings to prevent coal spillage and dust dispersion. Water spray equipment
is installed at the Unloader Hopper during handling to further prevent dust dispersion.

Water from spraying is collected into settling ponds and pumped back for treatment
without discharging to the sea. The company also continuously monitors seawater quality
around the coal handling port area of BLCP Power Plant.

Coal Yard

The coal yard can store 700,000 tons of coal, using 10,000 tons daily for electricity generation.

After coal is unloaded onshore, it is conveyed to the coal yard with 3 piles total.
The first 2 piles are used in rotation (Active Stock) for electricity generation,
while the last pile is reserved coal (Dead Stock) that is compacted for emergency fuel shortage.
Around all 3 coal piles, BLCP Power Plant has strict control measures to reduce environmental impact:

•Around the coal yard, 32 water spray systems are installed that can adjust water pressure
according to wind strength to prevent dust dispersion or heat combustion.
Water from coal pile spraying is collected to settling ponds before being recycled for further spraying.

•Wind Fence installation of 450 meters length and 6 meters height
to prevent direct wind impact on coal yard,
reducing dust dispersion or heat combustion.

•Dust monitoring equipment installed in 3 areas: north side, south side of coal yard,
and at Takwan-Ao Pradu community.

•Under the coal yard, High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) liner is installed
to prevent water seepage underground.
Water from coal pile spraying is collected to settling ponds before recycling for further spraying.
