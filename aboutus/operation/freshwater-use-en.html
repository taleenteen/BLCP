Water Management at BLCP Power Plant

For sustainability, the power plant employs efficient water management approaches as follows:

•Treated wastewater: The power plant reuses up to 1,500 cubic meters per day
of water that has been treated to meet quality standards suitable for system operations

•Freshwater from industrial estate: Receives water from Dok Krai and Nong Pla Lai reservoirs through pipeline systems
using approximately 700 cubic meters of freshwater per day, which is divided into:

  • • 300 cubic meters/day for office consumption and domestic use
  • • 2,100 cubic meters/day for electricity generation processes and dust control from coal yards and ash systems

(Note: Although the combined figures appear to exceed 2,200 cubic meters/day, in practice there is water recycling, and the average figures are estimates based on time periods)

Additionally, the power plant has installed
a seawater desalination system using Reverse Osmosis
(Desalination Reverse Osmosis)
with a production capacity of up to 4,000 cubic meters/day
This system can replace all raw water sources in case
of water shortage or drought conditions, helping the power plant operate continuously and securely in all circumstances
