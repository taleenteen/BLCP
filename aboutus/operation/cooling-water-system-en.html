Cooling Water System and Seawater Flue Gas Desulfurization (Sea Water FGD)

BLCP Power Plant uses approximately 5.34 million cubic meters of seawater per day for both generating units.
This seawater is utilized in two main systems:

  • 1.Cooling System
  • 2.Flue Gas Desulfurization (FGD) System

üîÑ Seawater Intake System

•Seawater is pumped through the Intake System at a low velocity of approximately 0.3 meters/second
to minimize impact on water currents and allow marine life to escape

•Two-stage filtration equipment installed:

  • • Bar Screen for trapping large debris
  • • Travelling Screen to prevent small fish or marine life from entering the system

•Debris from Bar Screen is disposed of together with other waste

•Marine life caught on Travelling Screen is washed off with water
and flows back to the sea through specially designed channels to minimize harm to marine life

üíß Water Quality Control in Cooling System

•Chlorine is added to prevent biofouling from marine organisms which could reduce the efficiency of the Condenser

•Chlorine concentration is controlled not to exceed 1.0 mg/L according to standard criteria

•When exiting the cooling system, residual chlorine is continuously destroyed through the FGD system and aeration pond
