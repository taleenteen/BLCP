Clean Coal Technology

Clean Coal Technology is an approach for developing and improving the process of using coal as fuel to achieve maximum benefits
while aiming to minimize environmental impact, including particulate matter, sulfur dioxide (SO‚ÇÇ), and nitrogen oxides (NO‚Çì).
This technology covers pre-combustion, during combustion, post-combustion, and coal conversion processes,
divided into 4 main stages as follows:

üßº Pre-combustion

This is the process of cleaning coal before use by removing impurities such as rock fragments, soil, and sulfur minerals to obtain cleaner coal and reduce pollution that would occur in subsequent stages.

•Methods include:

  • ► Physical methods (such as screening and size separation)
  • ► Chemical methods
  • ► Biological methods

üî• 2. Combustion

•May include the use of low-NOx burner technology or controlled temperature combustion.

üå´Ô∏è 3. Post-combustion

•Using Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) to reduce NO‚Çì

üîÑ 4. Coal Conversion

This is the process of converting coal into other forms of fuel, such as gas (Coal Gasification)

Power Plant Cycle

The power plant cycle is a process of circulating
thermal energy from fuel combustion
to create mechanical energy and convert it to
electrical energy using a steam cycle
with the following key components:

üîÅ Water Condensation and Temperature Increase

•Steam exiting the LP Turbine (Low Pressure Turbine) is condensed back to water by the Condenser
which uses seawater in a once-through cooling system as coolant

•The resulting water flows into the Condenser Hot Well and is pumped through:

  • ► Gland Condenser
  • ► Low Pressure Heater (to increase water temperature)
  • ► Then the hot water enters the De-aerator to remove dissolved gases such as oxygen

üí® Water Supply to Boiler Drum

•Water from the De-aerator is pumped by the Turbine Driven Boiler Feed Pump

•Through equipment:

  • ► High Pressure Heater
  • ► Economizer
    To further increase temperature before entering the Boiler Drum

üå°Ô∏è Steam and Hot Water Separation

•In the Boiler Drum, water is separated from steam

•Part of the water circulates through heating tubes back into the boiler

•Steam enters the Superheater Coil to increase temperature (becoming Superheated steam)

•Before entering the turbine, steam passes through temperature control with Spray Water Desuperheater

Main Steam and Reheat Steam

üöÄ Main Steam

•Steam with high temperature and pressure (Superheated Steam) passes through Governor Control Valves
to control the amount of steam entering the HP Turbine (High Pressure Turbine)

•HP Turbine converts thermal energy of steam into mechanical energy

üî• Reheat Steam

•Used steam from HP Turbine is sent back to the Boiler for Reheat (temperature increase again)

•Then enters IP Turbine (Intermediate Pressure) and LP Turbine (Low Pressure)

•Finally, steam flows through Crossover Pipe to the Condenser to condense back to water again

‚ö° Electricity Generation and Transmission

•Mechanical energy from steam turbines is transmitted to the Generator connected on the same shaft

•Generated electricity is sent to the 500 kV high voltage system of EGAT through:

  • • Isolated Phase Bus Duct
  • • Generator Transformer
  • • and Switchyard

‚úÖ Summary:

The power plant cycle is a continuous circular process of electricity generation
using steam as a medium for energy transfer from heat to efficient electricity production
with resource recovery and recycling (water) for cost-effective reuse in the system
