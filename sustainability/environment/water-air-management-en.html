Air Impact Reduction Measures

BLCP Power Plant prioritizes environmental management as a top priority and confirms
compliance with environmental standards, adhering to government regulations to help improve
environmental conditions. BLCP Power Plant uses proven technology from around the world
that is environmentally friendly, with the installation of various pollution reduction equipment including:

•Installation of Low NOx burners to reduce nitrogen oxide emissions

•Installation of Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for dust collection

•Installation of Flue Gas Desulphurization (FGD) system for sulfur dioxide removal

•200-meter high chimney stack

For air monitoring, BLCP Power Plant has installed Continuous Emission Monitoring System
(CEMS) at the chimney stack and 4 Air Quality Monitoring Stations (AQMS)
around the BLCP Power Plant area.

BLCP Power Plant
controls sulfur content in coal to low levels of 0.27-0.7%
with an annual average of 0.45%
and controls atmospheric emissions to levels better than
government standards as follows:

•Particulate matter: 43 mg/m3
from Pollution Control Department standard of 120 mg/m3
which is 64% better than standard

•Sulfur dioxide (SO2) as specified in EIA:
262 ppm from Pollution Control Department standard of 320
ppm, 18% better than standard

•Nitrogen oxides (NOx): 241 ppm
from Pollution Control Department standard of 350 ppm
31% better than standard

Water Impact Reduction Measures

Water used in the power plant is divided into 2 types:

• Fresh Water
(water used in production processes, port, coal yard, and offices) by
* Providing Waste Water Treatment Plant
* Water from port and coal yard is recycled for reuse

• Sea Water

Pumped in for cooling and sulfur dioxide treatment system, with installation of
2-layer screens (Traveling Screen and Bar Screen) to prevent marine life from entering the cooling system.
The seawater pumping speed for cooling is controlled not to exceed 0.3 meters per second.
These screens help marine life that may slip through
to easily exit the cooling water channel. Additionally, at the cooling water discharge point,
an aeration pond is installed to adjust temperature and pH before discharge to sea
(temperature < 40Â°C and pH ~ 7).
The system is regularly inspected with annual equipment maintenance plans, including continuous
monitoring of water quality and temperature around the BLCP Power Plant area.

Coal Ash

Coal ash is managed as follows:
All Fly Ash from combustion
is sold by BLCP Power Plant to cement industry
as a component in production, helping reduce raw material costs.
Bituminous coal ash is considered high-quality coal ash
and is popular both domestically and internationally,
such as being used in cement for constructing Khlong Tha Dan Dam
in Nakhon Nayok Province, which is a Royal Initiative project.
BLCP Power Plant therefore does not need to landfill any coal ash waste.

For Bottom Ash, in February 2025, a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) was signed
between BLCP Power Plant and Faculty of Architecture, Chulalongkorn University to develop
"temperature-reducing bricks" from bottom ash generated from electricity production process,
aiming to reduce waste volume and create products beneficial to society and environment.

These collaboratively developed temperature-reducing bricks have special properties to help reduce
indoor and surrounding temperatures, save energy, and are environmentally friendly.
They also align with Circular Economy principles that focus on valuable and sustainable resource utilization.

Coal Handling Port

Bituminous coal, mostly imported from Australia, is transported by ship to BLCP
Power Plant's deep-sea port. BLCP Power Plant emphasizes environmental protection measures
by inspecting the quality of imported bituminous coal on every ship voyage.

Coal handling into the power plant and coal yard is done using conveyor belts with
wind shields around them to prevent coal spillage and dust dispersion, with water spray
equipment installed during unloading at the Unloader Hopper to further prevent dust dispersion.

Water from spraying is collected into retention ponds and pumped back for treatment
without discharge to sea. Additionally, the company continuously monitors seawater quality
around the coal handling port area of BLCP Power Plant.

Coal Storage Yard

The coal storage yard can hold 700,000 tons of coal, using 10,000 tons daily for electricity generation.

After coal is unloaded onshore, it is conveyed to the coal storage yard with 3 piles total.
The first 2 piles are used in rotation (Active Stock) for electricity generation, while the last pile
is reserve coal (Dead Stock) that is compacted for use in emergency fuel shortage situations.
Around all 3 coal storage piles, BLCP Power Plant has strict control measures to reduce
environmental impact as follows:

Water from spraying is collected into retention ponds and pumped back for treatment
without discharge to sea. Additionally, the company continuously monitors seawater quality
around the coal handling port area of BLCP Power Plant.

•Around the coal storage yard, water spray systems are installed at 32 points that can adjust
water pressure according to wind strength to prevent dust dispersion or spontaneous combustion from heat.
Water from coal pile spraying is collected to sedimentation ponds before being recycled for further spraying.

•Installation of Wind Fence measuring 450 meters long and 6 meters high
to prevent wind from directly hitting the coal storage yard,
reducing dust dispersion or spontaneous combustion from heat.

•Installation of dust monitoring equipment in 3 areas: north side, south side of coal storage yard,
and at Takwan-Ao Pradu community.

•Beneath the coal storage yard, high-quality High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) liner is installed
to prevent water seepage underground.
Water from coal pile spraying is collected to sedimentation ponds before recycling for further spraying.
